What is Amoxicillin?

Amoxicillin is a broad-spectrum penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections including ear, nose, throat, urinary tract, and skin infections. It works by stopping bacterial cell wall synthesis, leading to bacterial cell death.

Each capsule contains 500mg of Amoxicillin trihydrate as the active ingredient, with microcrystalline cellulose, sodium starch glycolate, and magnesium stearate as excipients.

About PharmChain

Fighting Counterfeit Drugs with Blockchain

PharmChain is a distributed ledger platform designed to eliminate fake drugs from the pharmaceutical supply chain through cryptographic verification, real-time tracking, and transparent record-keeping.

The Problem

1 in 10 drugs in developing markets is fake or substandard

According to the WHO, approximately 10% of medical products in low- and middle-income countries are falsified or substandard. In Nigeria, NAFDAC estimates that up to 25% of drugs in circulation at any given time may not be genuine.

Counterfeit drugs kill hundreds of thousands of people annually ‚Äî and current supply chain systems lack the transparency, speed, and security needed to prevent this at scale.

Our Solution

Blockchain makes drug fraud mathematically impossible

PharmChain assigns every registered drug a unique cryptographic hash on a distributed blockchain ledger. Any tampering ‚Äî even changing a single character ‚Äî produces a completely different hash, instantly detectable.

From manufacturer to patient, every step is recorded, timestamped, and immutable. Anyone with a smartphone can verify a drug's complete history in under 2 seconds.
